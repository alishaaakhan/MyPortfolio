Madhya Pradesh is a state in central India. Its capital is Bhopal and the largest city is Indore, with Jabalpur, Gwalior, Ujjain and Sagar being the other major cities. Madhya Pradesh is the second largest state by area and the fifth largest state by population with over 72 million residents. It borders the states of Uttar Pradesh to the north, Chhattisgarh to the east, Maharashtra to the south, Gujarat to the west and Rajasthan to the northwest. The area covered by the present-day Madhya Pradesh includes the region of the ancient Avanti Mahajanapada, whose capital Ujjain (also known as Avantika) rose as a major city during the second wave of urbanisation in the 6th century BCE.

Madhya Pradesh literally means “Central Province”, and is located in the geographic heart of India. The state has a rich cultural heritage and is home to many historical monuments, temples, wildlife sanctuaries and beautiful tourist destinations. Because of its central location, it is often called the “Heart of Incredible India”.
