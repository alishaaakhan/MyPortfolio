Flower Gallery


Rose:

A classic, fragrant flower with thorny stems and layered petals available in many colors.


Lily:

A trumpet-shaped, highly fragrant flower that grows from bulbs.


Tulip:

A popular spring bulb with cup-shaped flowers that bloom on tall stems.


Sunflower:

A large, bright yellow flower
known for tracking the sun and producing seeds.


Daisy:

A simple, iconic flower with white petals surrounding a bright yellow center.


Orchid:

An exotic, intricate flower known for its long-lasting, elegant blooms.




Dahlia:

A dinner-plate-sized summer
flower with highly varied, intricate petal forms.


Hydrangea:

A shrub that produces large, puffy, rounded clusters of blue, pink, or white flowers.


Lavender:

A fragrant herb producing tiny, purple, spike-like aromatic
blooms.


Peony:

A lush, fluffy perennial with large, romantic double blooms in spring.


Marigold:

A bright, pom-pom-like annual flower often used for pest control
in gardens.


Carnation:

A ruffled-edged flower known for its long vase life and clove-like scent.




Jasmine:

A vining plant with intensely fragrant, small, star-shaped white flowers.


Iris:

A striking flower with three
upright petals and "sword-like" leaves.


Hibiscus:

A large, tropical plate-sized flower with a prominent stamen.


Chrysantheum:

A autumn-blooming flower with multiple spoon-shaped or ruffled petal varieties.


Gerbera Daisy:

A vibrant, large daisy-like flower featuring a daisy appearance in vivid colors.


Gladiolus:

A "sword lily" featuring tall,
spiked arrangements of stacked blooms.
